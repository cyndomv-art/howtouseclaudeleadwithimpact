Pro Tip  |  Watch Out For
💬
Chat
Where everyone starts. Where most people stop. Do not stop here — Chat is the on-ramp, not the destination.
Try this first
"Rewrite this email to sound confident and professional — clear, not cold."
Pro tip
Turn on Extended Thinking and Web Search before you start. Two clicks. Completely different results.
Watch out for
Re-explaining who you are every single session. Set up a Project once and never do it again.
📁
Projects
Your persistent workspace. Drop in your files, your role, your goals — Claude remembers so you do not have to repeat yourself.
Try this first
"Create a 'Workshop Facilitation' project. Upload your bio, audience profile, and past session notes. Every output will sound like you."
Pro tip
One Project per client, initiative, or topic. Focused context produces sharper, more relevant outputs every time.
Watch out for
Dumping every file you have into one Project. Five well-chosen documents beat fifty scattered ones.
⚡
Cowork
Claude stops answering and starts doing. Describe the outcome, step away, come back to finished work — real files, in your folder.
Try this first
"Here is a folder of notes from my last three sessions. Organize them into a summary report with key themes and action items."
Pro tip
Set Global Instructions once — your name, your role, your preferred format. Every Cowork session starts already informed.
Watch out for
Giving Claude a folder with no context. Tell it the goal before it starts — it plans better when it knows the destination.
💻
Code
The developer powerhouse — and the engine Cowork is built on. Claude writes, debugs, and ships code directly. If you do not code, know it exists. If your team builds tools, share this with them.
Try this first
"Ask your agency's developers if they are using Claude Code. If they are not, this is your conversation starter."
Pro tip
Understanding Code explains why Cowork is so capable. Same engine, friendlier interface. Knowing the connection makes you a smarter AI leader.
Watch out for
Assuming Code is not relevant to you. If your organization builds or maintains any internal tools, someone on your team needs to know this exists.
🎯
Skills
Your personal Claude playbook. Build it once, use it forever. Claude learns your standards — not just your preferences.
Try this first
"Think of one task you do every week. Write down exactly how you want it done. That is your first Skill."
Pro tip
Build a Skill for every task you repeat weekly — workshop outlines, executive summaries, email formats. One good Skill replaces an entire SOP.
Watch out for
Confusing Skills with Projects. Projects hold your context. Skills teach Claude your process. You need both.
🔗
Connectors
Claude inside the tools you already use. Google Drive, Slack, Notion, and 50+ more. Free to connect. No switching tabs.
Try this first
"Find my most recent workshop outline in Google Drive and pull the key talking points."
Pro tip
Connectors work across Chat, Projects, and Cowork. Connect once — Claude searches on demand across all three surfaces.
Watch out for
Thinking Connectors sync automatically. Claude searches your tools when you ask — not continuously in the background.
🔌
Plugins
Pre-built power-ups for your role. Install one and Claude shows up as a specialist — ready with the right commands for how you work.
Try this first
"Install a plugin for your field. Type / in any chat to see every command available."
Pro tip
Start with two plugins that match your actual job. Focused context outperforms having everything installed at once.
Watch out for
Installing every plugin at once. Claude performs best with focused, relevant context — not everything at the same time.
🔒
Security First
Before anything else — know what never goes in. AI tools are powerful. Protecting sensitive information is non-negotiable.
The rule
"Would I post this publicly?" If no — do not put it in AI. Use hypothetical scenarios and anonymized examples instead.
Pro tip
Never input PII, personnel records, case numbers, or anything marked sensitive — even in a "private" chat. Use fictional stand-ins always.
Watch out for
Assuming "private" means protected. Default to caution. Your judgment is the last line of defense — not the platform.
